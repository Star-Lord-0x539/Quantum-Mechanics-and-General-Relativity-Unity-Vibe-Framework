\section{The de~Sitter tensor completion: the audited-minimal form
  (E0-dS$'$)}
\label{app:e0ds-min}

A proof-level audit of every displayed consumer of
Hyp.~\ref{hyp:E0-dS} shows that the ball-modular family of every
radius and centre --- the Casini--Huerta--Myers scanning
structure~\cite{CasiniHuertaMyers2011} --- is the shape of the
AdS-anchored \emph{route} (Thm.~\ref{thm:E0-lin}), not of the
framework's need: what the printed inversion steps consume is the
pointwise null--null equation of state at every $(x,k)$, Hadamard
conservation, the linearised Bianchi identity, and the posited
$\Lambda$ seam. The following three lemmas show that this consumed
strength follows from per-cut first laws on the $\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$
orbit of static-patch wedges of $\mathrm{dS}_4$; the audited-minimal
hypothesis (E0-dS$'$) then replaces the ball family. Throughout,
$\mathrm{dS}_4\subset\mathbb R^{1,4}$ is the unit hyperboloid.

\begin{lemma}[null coverage by the wedge orbit]
\label{lem:e0ds-coverage}
Let $x\in\mathrm{dS}_4$ and let $k\in T_x\mathrm{dS}_4$ be null. Then
there is a wedge $W$ in the $\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$ orbit of the static
patch such that $x$ lies on the bifurcation surface $\partial W$ of
its bifurcate Killing horizon and $k$ is a null normal of
$\partial W$ at $x$ (the generator direction of one horizon sheet).
The wedges with this property form a $2$-parameter family.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The orthogonal complement $k^{\perp}\subset T_x\mathrm{dS}_4$ is a
degenerate $3$-plane containing $k$; its lifts to spacelike
$2$-planes $P\subset k^{\perp}$ transverse to $k$ form a
$2$-parameter family. For any such $P$, the totally geodesic surface
$\Sigma=\mathrm{dS}_4\cap\Pi$, with $\Pi\subset\mathbb R^{1,4}$ the
linear $3$-plane with $T_x\Sigma=P$, is a totally geodesic round
$2$-sphere through $x$ whose normal bundle at $x$ is the Lorentzian
$2$-plane $P^{\perp}\ni k$, containing exactly two null rays, one of
them $\mathbb Rk$. The subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$ fixing $\Sigma$
up to rotation contains a boost whose bifurcate Killing horizon has
bifurcation surface $\Sigma$ and generators along the null normals;
its wedge is an $\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$-image of the static patch, since
$\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$ acts transitively on (totally geodesic bifurcation
sphere, null normal) data.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[null--null determination plus identities; trace closure
  without trace-channel data]
\label{lem:e0ds-trace}
Let $E_{\mu\nu}$ be a symmetric tensor field on a region of an
\emph{on-shell} semiclassical background
($G^{(0)}_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda g^{(0)}_{\mu\nu}
=\kappa\langle T^{(0)}_{\mu\nu}\rangle_{\rm BD}$, equivalently
$\langle T\rangle$ taken Bunch--Davies-relative so that the
background combination $E^{(0)}=0$; the background field equations
are consumed here --- they are what make $E_{\mu\nu}$
gauge-consistent across the per-wedge adapted frames, by
Stewart--Walker invariance of the linearised combination, which
requires $E^{(0)}=0$), of the
form $E=\delta G+\Lambda\,\delta g-\kappa\,\delta\langle T\rangle$
with $\nabla^{\mu}\delta\langle T_{\mu\nu}\rangle=0$ (Hadamard
conservation). If $E_{\mu\nu}k^{\mu}k^{\nu}=0$ for every null $k$ at
every point, then $E_{\mu\nu}=c\,g^{(0)}_{\mu\nu}$ with $c$
\emph{constant}: the linearised Einstein equation holds up to a
shift of $\Lambda$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Pointwise, a symmetric tensor annihilated by all null--null
contractions is proportional to the metric (polarisation over the
null cone; the local-Rindler move of step~(i) of the printed
inversion remark, \cite{Jacobson1995}). So $E=c(x)\,g^{(0)}$. The
linearised contracted Bianchi identity about an Einstein background
gives $\nabla^{\mu}(\delta G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda\,\delta g_{\mu\nu})=0$
identically; with matter conservation,
$0=\nabla^{\mu}E_{\mu\nu}=\partial_{\nu}c$. The constant $c$ is the
trace-value seam of step~(iv) of the printed inversion: it
renormalises $\Lambda$ and is posited separately, exactly as in
print. The trace part of the \emph{equation} thus closes by
identities alone, consuming no modular data at any scale.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[second cut-variation: the smeared-to-pointwise step,
  with its cost stated]
\label{lem:e0ds-cut}
Fix a wedge $W$ as in Lem.~\ref{lem:e0ds-coverage}, horizon
coordinates $(v,\Omega)$ with affine $v$ along the generators.
Suppose: (a) for every cut $V(\cdot)$ in a $C^{2}$ neighbourhood of
the bifurcation cut the \emph{equality-strength} first law holds at
first order in the perturbation, with the geometric pairing
\[
\frac{\delta\bigl[A_{\infty}-A(V)\bigr]}{4G}\;=\;\delta S(V)
\;=\;\delta\langle
K_{V}\rangle \;=\;2\pi\!\int\!d\Omega\,\sqrt h
\int_{V(\Omega)}^{\infty}\!\!dv\,\bigl(v-V(\Omega)\bigr)\,
\delta\langle T_{vv}(v,\Omega)\rangle,
\]
both sides twice differentiable in $V$ (the geometric side is the
\emph{future-normalised area deficit} --- the literal $\delta A(V)$
reading returns the anti-Einstein sign under the second
cut-variation; $A_{\infty}$ drops out of both variations); (b) the \emph{first moment}
$\int dv\,(v-V)\,|\delta\langle T_{vv}\rangle|$ is finite on every
complete generator (an existence premise for the display, strictly
stronger than the averaged-null-energy pin the paper elsewhere
assumes); and (c) for cut vector fields with nonvanishing
$\xi$ the geometric side is read as the first-order \emph{Wald}
entropy variation --- for the improved stress tensor the improvement
term in $T_{vv}$ equals the first-order Wald surface term exactly,
so the pairing is with Wald entropy and no improvement ambiguity
leaks into the display, the effective coupling being
$1/G_{\rm eff}=1/G-8\pi\xi\langle\varphi^{2}\rangle_{\rm BD}$
(spacetime-constant on $\mathrm{dS}$, so the $\Lambda$ seam and the
Bianchi identity are intact; the correction rescales the coefficient,
not the form). Then $\delta^{2}/\delta V(\Omega)^{2}$
yields the pointwise null--null equation of state
$\delta G_{kk}(x)=8\pi G_{\rm eff}\,\delta\langle T_{kk}(x)\rangle$ at every
point of every generator (matter side:
$\delta^{2}\langle K_{V}\rangle/\delta V^{2}
=2\pi\,\delta\langle T_{vv}(V,\Omega)\rangle\sqrt h$; geometric
side: linearised Raychaudhuri, $\partial_{v}\delta\theta
=-\delta R_{vv}$, integrated against the same weight). Combined with
Lem.~\ref{lem:e0ds-coverage} and Lem.~\ref{lem:e0ds-trace} this
gives the linearised tensor equation about $\mathrm{dS}_4$ up to the
$\Lambda$ seam.
\end{lemma}

\begin{hypothesis}[(E0-dS$'$): cut-localised first law on the
  de~Sitter wedge orbit --- the audited-minimal form]
\label{hyp:E0-dS-min}
On the $\mathrm{SO}(1,4)$ orbit of static-patch wedges of
$\mathrm{dS}_4$, in a Bunch--Davies-class Hadamard state of the
massive (SM) matter net, the equality-strength first law of
Lem.~\ref{lem:e0ds-cut} --- with its premises (a)--(c), including
the finite first moment per generator --- holds for every cut in a
$C^{2}$ neighbourhood of the bifurcation cut of every wedge, at the
order in the couplings at which the tensor equation is asserted.
\end{hypothesis}

\label{rem:e0ds-min-status}

displayed consumer of Hyp.~\ref{hyp:E0-dS} uses; the printed

and the per-cut pairing (App.~\ref{app:ds-pairing}: exact horizon

completion re-pins to the A3 obstruction and stays walled --- and

route never consumes: $k^{2}=0$ annihilates the trace channel on

wedge orbit (every bifurcation surface is a great sphere pinned at

identities of Lem.~\ref{lem:e0ds-trace}.
\end{remark}
