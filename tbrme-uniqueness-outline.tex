\begin{abstract}
This companion paper proves a uniqueness theorem for the
Two-Boundary Retrocausal Master Equation (TBRME) of the main
unification paper. Under seven explicit axioms on a
densely-defined symmetric constraint operator $\widehat C$ on
the universal Hilbert space $\mathcal U$ --- clock covariance
with the fibrewise Acz\'el--d'Alembert cocycle, modular
covariance with respect to the \emph{non-tracial} dual seed
state $\hat\omega$ on the CLPW Type~II$_1$ gravity sector,
direct-sum locality in the branch index, TDSE flat-space limit,
semiclassical-Einstein WKB limit, a pure-geometry
\emph{universality-class} condition in the sense of
Kaimakkamis--Sil, and branch-uniform Part~I normalization ---
we show $\widehat C$ equals $\lambda\,\widehat{\mathfrak C}_k$
uniquely up to (a) a single overall positive scale $\lambda$,
(b) conjugation by unitaries of the $\hat\omega$-centralizer
$\ctr{\MM_{\rm geom}}$, and (c) von~Neumann
self-adjoint-extension freedom where the deficiency indices of
$\widehat C\!\restriction_\Phi$ are non-trivial and equal. The
structural template is Giulini--Marolf rigging-map uniqueness
(outer frame; it also sources the per-branch scale freedom that
Axiom~X7 collapses), with the scalar Acz\'el--d'Alembert
classification supplying the clock factor (Kisy\'nski--Sova
operator-cosine theory certifying the generator), the
Pedersen--Takesaki noncommutative Radon--Nikodym theorem
supplying the gravity factor on the finite-trace Type~II$_1$
sector, and Meda--Pinamonti--Siemssen semiclassical
well-posedness closing the WKB leg. All external ingredients
are in the ambient literature and are cited at theorem level;
the contribution of this paper is assembly, an axiom
independence/consistency audit, the over-determination lemma
for the matter--gravity coupling, and explicit identification
of the three irreducible ambiguity classes.
\end{abstract}

\section{Standing structures and axioms}
\label{sec:axioms}

Let $\mathcal U=\bigoplus_{k\in\KK_+}\HH_{\rm clock}\otimes
\HH_{\rm geom}^{(k)}\otimes\HH_{\rm matter}^{(k)}$, where each
$\HH_{\rm geom}^{(k)}=L^2(\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)},\tr_k)$ carries
the CLPW/DEHK crossed-product structure of App.~F of the main
paper in its corrected form: for a positive clock Hamiltonian
on $L^2(\RR_+)$ and compact region, $\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}$ is a
\emph{Type~II$_1$ factor} with normalized finite trace $\tr_k$,
$\tr_k(\id)=1$~\cite{CLPW2022,PedersenTakesaki1973}. Let
$\hat\omega_k=\tr_k(h_k\,\cdot\,)$ be the dual state induced by
the branch seed $\omega_k$, with Radon--Nikodym density
$h_k\in L^1(\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)},\tr_k)_+$, $h_k$ injective
(faithfulness), $\tr_k(h_k)=1$. Its modular automorphism group
is \emph{inner},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:inner-modular}
  \sigma^{\hat\omega_k}_s\;=\;\mathrm{Ad}\bigl(h_k^{\,is}\bigr),
\end{equation}
by the Pedersen--Takesaki Radon--Nikodym theorem for semifinite
algebras~\cite{PedersenTakesaki1973} (equivalently: a von
Neumann algebra is semifinite iff its modular flows are inner).
In particular the modular group of the \emph{trace} is trivial,
$\sigma^{\tr_k}=\mathrm{id}$; all modular-covariance statements
below are therefore made with respect to $\hat\omega_k$, never
$\tr_k$.

Let $\widehat C$ be a densely-defined \emph{symmetric} operator
on a common invariant rigged-Hilbert dense domain
$\Phi\subset\mathcal U$ (essential self-adjointness is
\emph{not} assumed; see ambiguity (c) and
Rem.~\ref{rem:esa-vs-c}). Assume:

\begin{axiom}[(X1) Clock covariance and Acz\'el--d'Alembert
cocycle]\label{ax:X1}
$[\widehat C,\widehat H_{\rm c}\otimes\id\otimes\id]=0$ on
$\Phi$, where $\widehat H_{\rm c}$ is the Page--Wootters clock
generator, and the clock-shift response
$B(\delta):=\widehat\Sigma$-part of $\widehat C$ under finite
clock shifts satisfies, on the band-limited subspace
$\HH_{[E_\star]}$, the package of the main paper's
Prop.~A (operator-sense d'Alembert reduction): $B(\delta)$
bounded, self-adjoint, even in $\delta$, strongly continuous
in $\delta$ on $\mathcal D(\widehat H_{\rm c}^2)$, with
$B(\delta)\in\{\widehat H_{\rm c}\}''$ and the inhomogeneous
cocycle
\[
  B(\delta_1+\delta_2)+B(\delta_1-\delta_2)
  =2\cos(\delta_1\widehat H_{\rm c}/\hbar)B(\delta_2)
  +2B(\delta_1),
\]
together with $\mu_{\widehat H_{\rm c}}$-measurability of the
fibrewise data $\xi\mapsto f(\xi,\delta)$ for each rational
$\delta$.
\end{axiom}

\begin{remark}[A29 accommodation]\label{rem:A29}
X1 deliberately states the \emph{inhomogeneous} cocycle plus
the measurability/continuity hypotheses under which the
fibrewise scalar Acz\'el classification is a theorem (main
paper, Prop.~\emph{wdw-dalembert} and
Thm.~\emph{wdw-selection}); no homogeneous operator-cosine
hypothesis on $C=B+\id$ is made or used. If the sibling
re-route adds further measurability hypotheses, they are to be
absorbed into X1 verbatim.
\end{remark}

\begin{axiom}[(X2) Modular covariance w.r.t.\ the dual seed
state, non-degenerate]\label{ax:X2}
For each branch $k$ there is a decomposition on $\Phi$,
$\widehat C_k=\id\otimes\widehat C_{\rm grav}^{(k)}\otimes\id
+\widehat C_{\rm rest}^{(k)}$, with
$\widehat C_{\rm grav}^{(k)}$ symmetric and affiliated to
$\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}$, $\widehat C_{\rm rest}^{(k)}$
commuting with $h_k^{\,is}$ for all $s$, such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:X2}
  \mathrm{Ad}\bigl(e^{it\widehat C_{\rm grav}^{(k)}}\bigr)
  \restriction_{\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}}
  \;=\;\sigma^{\hat\omega_k}_{-\lambda_k t}
  \qquad\text{for some }\lambda_k>0\text{ and all }t\in\RR .
\end{equation}
\emph{Non-degeneracy:} $\hat\omega_k$ is non-tracial, i.e.\
$h_k\notin\CC\,\id$. (If $h_k$ were scalar,
$\sigma^{\hat\omega_k}=\mathrm{id}$ and \eqref{eq:X2} would be
vacuous; the axiom then selects nothing. Non-degeneracy holds
automatically whenever the seed modular Hamiltonian
$K_{\omega_k}$ is non-scalar, i.e.\ in every physical branch.)
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[(X3) Direct-sum locality in the branch
index]\label{ax:X3}
$\widehat C=\bigoplus_{k\in\KK_+}\widehat C_k$, with each
$\widehat C_k$ affiliated to $\mathcal B(\HH_{\rm clock})
\otimes\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}\otimes\mathcal B(\HH_{\rm matter}
^{(k)})$, where $\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}$ is the Type~II$_1$
crossed-product factor above.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[(X4) TDSE flat-space limit, projective
form]\label{ax:X4}
In the flat, classical-clock, finite-matter limit (reduction
(U1)/(U2) of the main paper), $\widehat C_k\downarrow
c_k\bigl(\widehat H_{\rm clock}+\widehat H_{\rm matter}^{(k)}
\bigr)$ for some $c_k>0$, strongly on the limit domain.
\emph{(Projective form: only the ray of the limit generator is
fixed. The strict form $c_k=1$ would force $\lambda=1$ in
Thm.~\ref{thm:main} and render ambiguity (a) vacuous; see
Sec.~\ref{sec:audit}.)} The limit additionally supplies the
TDSE normalization $-B''(0)=\alpha\widehat H_{\rm c}^2/\hbar^2$
on $\mathcal D(\widehat H_{\rm c}^2)$ used to fix the common
fibrewise scale in the clock sector.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[(X5) Semiclassical-Einstein WKB
limit]\label{ax:X5}
In the WKB-geometry sector, $\langle\widehat C\rangle$-vanishing
yields the semiclassical Einstein equation
$G_{\mu\nu}=(8\pi G/c^4)\langle T_{\mu\nu}\rangle$ (reduction
(U3) of the main paper), within the scope where
Meda--Pinamonti--Siemssen local existence--uniqueness
holds~\cite{MedaPinamontiSiemssen2020}: \emph{flat} FLRW
backgrounds driven by a massive scalar with arbitrary curvature
coupling, initial data at finite time, regular compatible
state. (The earlier ``compact-Cauchy FLRW'' scope and the
``Pinamonti--Drago--Hack'' attribution were incorrect and are
withdrawn.)
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[(X6) Pure-geometry universality class
(weakened)]\label{ax:X6}
Tracing out $\HH_{\rm clock}\otimes\HH_{\rm matter}^{(k)}$
reduces $\widehat C_k$ to a quantum Hamiltonian constraint
whose (i) classical limit is the ADM/Wheeler--DeWitt constraint
of general relativity, and whose (ii) operator-ordering
ambiguity function lies in the \emph{same Kaimakkamis--Sil
universality class}~\cite{KaimakkamisSil2024} as the minimal
(Laplace--Beltrami-covariant) ordering: under the
field-redefinition-invariant reformulation, all ordering
ambiguities consolidate into a single higher-order scalar
function on minisuperspace, and X6 requires only membership of
the minimal class. \emph{X6 makes no reference to the cosine
deformation $\widehat\Sigma_\delta$ or to any other structure
that Thm.~\ref{thm:main} is supposed to deliver; the
$\Sigma_\delta$ term is derived from X1+X4, not assumed here.}
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[(X7) Branch-uniform Part~I
normalization]\label{ax:X7}
On finite-dimensional matter factors, $\widehat C_k$ restricts
to the Part~I two-boundary constraint (reduction (U2) of the
main paper) \emph{with a branch-independent normalization}:
the constants $c_k$ of X4 and the flow rescalings $\lambda_k$
of X2 satisfy $c_k=c$, $\lambda_k=\lambda$ for all
$k\in\KK_+$. Equivalently: the Giulini--Marolf per-sector
scale freedom~\cite{GiuliniMarolf1999} is fixed to a single
global scale by Part~I consistency of the branch weights
$w(k)$.
\end{axiom}

\begin{remark}[What X7 is for]\label{rem:X7-role}
Giulini--Marolf uniqueness is \emph{per superselection
sector}: on a direct sum the rigging map (and with it the
normalization of the constraint) is unique only up to one
positive constant per sector~\cite{GiuliniMarolf1999}. Under
X3 every branch $k$ is a superselection sector, so X1--X6
alone can at best give $\widehat C_k=\lambda_k\widehat{
\mathfrak C}_k$ with an uncontrolled sequence $(\lambda_k)$.
X7's entire job is to collapse $(\lambda_k)\to\lambda$; it is
\emph{not} an independent rigidity input on the operator form
(that part of the old X7 was implied by X4 and has been
removed --- see Sec.~\ref{sec:audit}).
\end{remark}

\section{Main theorem}
\label{sec:main-theorem}

\begin{theorem}[TBRME uniqueness, sharp form]\label{thm:main}
Under Ax.~\ref{ax:X1}--\ref{ax:X7} on the universal Hilbert
space $\mathcal U$ of the main paper,
\[
  \widehat C\;=\;\lambda\,\bigoplus_{k\in\KK_+}
  \widehat{\mathfrak C}_k
  \qquad\text{for some }\lambda\in\RR_{>0},
\]
uniquely up to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
  \item[(\textbf{a})] the overall positive scalar $\lambda>0$
    (single and global, by X7; per-branch scales $\lambda_k$
    if X7 is dropped);
  \item[(\textbf{b})] conjugation by unitaries of the
    $\hat\omega_k$-centralizers
    $\ctr{\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}}=\{h_k\}'\cap\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}$
    (a genuine gauge class: such conjugations fix the clock and
    gravity generators but act non-trivially on the
    gravity--matter coupling ordering);
  \item[(\textbf{c})] a von~Neumann self-adjoint-extension
    choice when the deficiency indices $(n_+,n_-)$ of
    $\widehat C\!\restriction_\Phi$ are non-trivial and equal
    \cite{ReedSimonII}. \emph{(Attribution corrected: this
    freedom is von~Neumann extension theory, not
    Giulini--Marolf~\cite{GiuliniMarolf1999}, whose theorem
    concerns rigging-map scale per superselection sector and
    contains no deficiency-index content.)}
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Essential self-adjointness vs.\ ambiguity
(c)]\label{rem:esa-vs-c}
The first scaffold assumed $\widehat C$ essentially
self-adjoint \emph{and} kept ambiguity (c); that conjunction is
inconsistent (essential self-adjointness means
$(n_+,n_-)=(0,0)$). The standing assumption is now symmetry on
$\Phi$ only. In the regime where the main paper's Kato--Rellich
argument (T4)+(T7) applies, $\widehat C\!\restriction_\Phi$ is
essentially self-adjoint and (c) collapses; (c) is live
precisely outside that regime (e.g.\ at minisuperspace
boundaries $a\to0$), see Sec.~\ref{sec:GM-extension}.
\end{remark}

\section{Proof architecture}
\label{sec:proof}

\subsection{Clock sector: fibrewise Acz\'el--d'Alembert
closure}\label{sec:proof-clock}

X1 (covariance $+$ spectral theorem) forces
$B(\delta)=f(\widehat H_{\rm c},\delta)$; the cocycle read on
each spectral fibre $\xi$ is the \emph{scalar} inhomogeneous
d'Alembert equation, whose continuous even solutions with
$f(\xi,0)=0$ are $f(\xi,\delta)=c(\xi)(\cos(\delta\xi/\hbar)-1)$
(Acz\'el~\cite{Aczel1966}); measurability of $c(\xi)$ from X1's
measurability clause; the TDSE normalization inside X4 forces
$c(\xi)\equiv\alpha$. Kisy\'nski--Sova operator-cosine theory
\cite{Kisynski1972,Sova1966} enters only to certify that the
normalized member is the cosine function of
$A=(\widehat H_{\rm c}/\hbar)^2\succeq0$.
\emph{Import from App.~A of the main paper as corrected under
A29; do not import the withdrawn $C=B+\id$ homogeneous-cosine
step.} \PROVEN{} on $\HH_{[E_\star]}$;
extension to unbounded $\widehat H_{\rm c}$ without band-limit:
\OPEN{} (tedious spectral-limit argument, expected routine).

\subsection{Gravity sector: Pedersen--Takesaki rigidity on the
Type~II$_1$ factor}\label{sec:proof-gravity}

The Takesaki--Connes cocycle-derivative theorem
\cite[Thm.~VIII.3.3]{Takesaki2003} is type-agnostic and
survives the reclassification; but on a II$_1$ factor it must
be deployed against the non-tracial state $\hat\omega_k$, since
$\sigma^{\tr}=\mathrm{id}$. On the finite-trace sector the
argument in fact simplifies to the following elementary lemma.

\begin{lemma}[Gravity-sector rigidity, II$_1$
form]\label{lem:gravity-rigidity}
Let $\MM$ be a II$_1$ factor with normalized trace $\tr$, and
$\hat\omega=\tr(h\,\cdot\,)$ a faithful normal non-tracial
state, $h\in L^1(\MM,\tr)_+$ injective, $\tr(h)=1$. Suppose
$\widehat C_{\rm grav}$ is self-adjoint, affiliated to $\MM$,
and satisfies \eqref{eq:X2}:
$\mathrm{Ad}(e^{it\widehat C_{\rm grav}})\restriction_\MM
=\sigma^{\hat\omega}_{-\lambda t}=\mathrm{Ad}(h^{-i\lambda t})$.
Then
\[
  \widehat C_{\rm grav}\;=\;\lambda\,(-\log h)\;+\;\mu\,\id,
  \qquad \mu\in\RR,
\]
and $\mu=0$ iff $\tr\!\bigl(e^{-\widehat C_{\rm grav}/\lambda}
\bigr)=1$. In particular the II$_1$ trace normalization
$\tr(\id)=1$, $\tr(h)=1$ \emph{canonically fixes the additive
constant}, a strengthening unavailable in the II$_\infty$
setting.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Set $u_t:=e^{it\widehat C_{\rm grav}}\,h^{\,i\lambda t}$. By
hypothesis $\mathrm{Ad}(u_t)=\mathrm{id}$ on $\MM$, so
$u_t\in\MM'\cap\MM=\CC\id$ ($\MM$ a factor; $u_t\in\MM$ since
both factors are affiliated to $\MM$). Thus
$e^{it\widehat C_{\rm grav}}=c(t)\,h^{-i\lambda t}$ with
$c:\RR\to\mathbb T$; strong continuity and the group law give
$c(t)=e^{i\mu t}$, and Stone's theorem yields
$\widehat C_{\rm grav}=-\lambda\log h+\mu\id$ on the common
core. The normalization claim is functional calculus plus
$\tr(h)=1$.
\end{proof}

\PROVABLE{} (the sketch is complete modulo domain bookkeeping
for the affiliated logarithm, which is standard in
$L^1(\MM,\tr)$).

\begin{remark}[Where the centralizer ambiguity (b) actually
lives]\label{rem:centralizer}
Lemma~\ref{lem:gravity-rigidity} pins
$\widehat C_{\rm grav}^{(k)}$ \emph{exactly} (up to
$\lambda_k$); conjugation by a unitary
$u\in\ctr{\MM_{\rm geom}^{(k)}}$ fixes $h_k$ (hence
$\widehat C_{\rm grav}^{(k)}$) and fixes
$\sigma^{\hat\omega_k}$, but acts non-trivially on the
gravity--matter coupling term $\widehat Q^{(k)}[f]$. Ambiguity
(b) is therefore \emph{not} a residual freedom of the gravity
generator (as the first scaffold suggested); it is a residual
gauge action on the \emph{coupling ordering}. In a II$_1$
factor the centralizer $\{h\}'\cap\MM$ is typically large, so
(b) is a genuinely non-trivial class; partial collapse under
X5 is discussed in Sec.~\ref{sec:proof-matter}, step (v).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[II$_\infty$ variant]\label{rem:IIinfty-variant}
On non-compact sectors (black-hole exterior, CPW
\cite{CPW2022}) $\MM$ is II$_\infty$ with semifinite trace;
Lemma~\ref{lem:gravity-rigidity} survives verbatim except that
the density $h$ is $\tr$-measurable with possibly
$\tr(h)=\infty$-adjacent normalization, and the additive
constant $\mu$ is no longer canonically fixed. The theorem
statement is uniform: ``Type~II factor with faithful normal
semifinite trace, finite (and normalized) in the
positive-clock/compact case.''
\end{remark}

\subsection{Matter sector: the over-determination
lemma}\label{sec:proof-matter}

After Secs.~\ref{sec:proof-clock}--\ref{sec:proof-gravity} and
X3, the residual freedom in $\widehat C_k$ is: the pure-matter
part, the gravity--matter coupling ordering in
$\widehat Q^{(k)}[f]$, and centralizer conjugation (b).

\begin{lemma}[Over-determination of the coupling
ordering]\label{lem:overdetermination}
Let $\widehat C,\widehat C'$ both satisfy X1--X7 and define
$D_k:=\widehat C_k-\widehat C'_k$ on $\Phi$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*,nosep]
  \item \textup{(Clock.)} The clock parts of $\widehat
    C_k,\widehat C'_k$ coincide up to the global scale; $D_k$
    commutes with the clock algebra.
    \PROVEN{} (Sec.~\ref{sec:proof-clock}).
  \item \textup{(Gravity.)} The gravity parts differ by
    $(\lambda-\lambda')(-\log h_k)$ only; no centralizer term
    survives in the generator itself.
    \PROVABLE{} (Lemma~\ref{lem:gravity-rigidity}).
  \item \textup{(Pure matter.)} X4 (projective TDSE limit)
    forces the pure-matter parts onto the same ray:
    the flat-limit reduction of $D_k$ is
    $(c_k-c_k')(\widehat H_{\rm clock}+\widehat H_{\rm matter}
    ^{(k)})$, i.e.\ scale only. \PROVABLE{} (strong-limit
    bookkeeping on the limit domain).
  \item \textup{(WKB order.)} X5 forces the leading WKB symbol
    of the coupling part of $D_k$ to vanish: both constraints
    reduce to semiclassical Einstein flows through the
    \emph{same} initial data, and Meda--Pinamonti--Siemssen
    uniqueness~\cite{MedaPinamontiSiemssen2020} of the
    semiclassical solution forces the two flows --- hence the
    two leading symbols --- to coincide, fixing in particular
    the coupling constant $\kappa^{-1}$ relative to the matter
    normalization of (iii). \PARTIAL{} (MPS supply forward
    well-posedness as a theorem; the ``equal limits $+$ unique
    flow $\Rightarrow$ equal leading symbols'' step is an
    inverse-direction argument that must be written; expected
    provable on the MPS scope, i.e.\ flat FLRW + massive
    scalar).
  \item \textup{(Ordering class.)} X6 places the pure-geometry
    reductions of both constraints in the Kaimakkamis--Sil
    minimal universality class~\cite{KaimakkamisSil2024}; the
    within-class freedom is, by their field-redefinition-
    invariant reformulation, a change of inner-product measure
    / field redefinition, i.e.\ unitarily implemented gauge ---
    and the unitaries in question that preserve X2 lie in the
    centralizer, so the within-class freedom is \emph{absorbed
    into ambiguity (b)}. \PARTIAL{}: proven by
    \cite{KaimakkamisSil2024} for minisuperspace models
    (1-dim and $n$-dim with scalar matter); the lift to the
    midisuperspace/QFT matter content of the main paper is
    \OPEN{} (this is open problem O1, the principal
    conjectural input of the companion paper).
  \item \textup{(Residual $=$ extension data.)} What survives
    (i)--(v) is supported on minisuperspace boundary terms
    (e.g.\ $a\to0$): symmetric, annihilated by all interior
    limits, hence localized in the deficiency subspaces of
    $\widehat C_k\!\restriction_\Phi$. This is exactly the
    von~Neumann extension freedom, ambiguity (c). \OPEN{}
    (conjecture O2: ``interior-trivial $\Rightarrow$
    boundary-supported'' needs a precise boundary-triple
    formulation; plausible via standard boundary-triple theory
    on the DeWitt-metric minisuperspace, but not yet written).
\end{enumerate}
Consequently $\widehat C_k=\lambda_k\widehat{\mathfrak C}_k$
modulo (b) and (c), with $(\lambda_k)$ per-branch.
\end{lemma}

\subsection{Assembly}\label{sec:proof-assembly}

X3 assembles the branch constraints as a direct sum. Each
branch is a superselection sector; Giulini--Marolf
\cite{GiuliniMarolf1999} uniqueness of the rigging map holds
per sector up to a positive scale, so the assembly step alone
yields $(\lambda_k)_{k\in\KK_+}$. X7 collapses
$\lambda_k\equiv\lambda$, which is ambiguity (a). \PROVABLE{}
(bookkeeping once (i)--(vi) are in place).

\section{Self-adjoint-extension discussion (ambiguity
(c))}\label{sec:GM-extension}

Spell out when deficiency indices can be non-trivial for
$\widehat C\!\restriction_\Phi$: in the Kato--Rellich regime of
the main paper's (T4)+(T7) the constraint is essentially
self-adjoint and (c) collapses; candidate non-trivial cases are
minisuperspace boundaries ($a\to0$) and non-compact matter
sectors. Physical principles that collapse (c): positive clock
spectrum, positivity of the semiclassical energy, DeWitt
boundary condition. \emph{Cite von~Neumann extension theory
\cite[Thm.~X.2]{ReedSimonII} and, for the constraint-quantization
frame, Marolf's single-constraint RAQ~\cite{GiuliniMarolf1999};
the former ``Giulini--Marolf case (iii)'' citation is withdrawn
--- no such enumeration exists in gr-qc/9902045.}

\section{Relation to the main paper's App.~A}
\label{sec:vs-kisynski}

The clock-shift cocycle $\widehat\Sigma_\delta=
\cos(\delta\widehat H_{\rm c}/\hbar)-\id$ of the main paper's
clock-sector uniqueness (App.~A, as corrected under A29:
fibrewise Acz\'el selection, Kisy\'nski--Sova certification) is
the clock-sector projection of Thm.~\ref{thm:main}. The present
paper extends that closure from the clock factor to the full
universal Hilbert space $\mathcal U$.

\section{Axiom audit: dependency, independence,
consistency}\label{sec:audit}

\subsection*{Dependency diagram}

\subsection*{Independence and non-redundancy}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
  \item \textbf{X4 vs X7 (redundancy found and removed).} The
    old X7 (``restricts to the Part~I constraint on finite-dim
    factors'') was, at the level of the constraint
    \emph{operator}, implied by X4: the flat classical-clock
    limit through finite-dimensional matter factors already
    fixes the Part~I generator. The non-redundant content of
    X7 is only the branch-\emph{uniform} normalization (and the
    boundary-data/Born-weight structure, which concerns the
    state space, not $\widehat C$, and is moved out of the
    axiom set). X7 has been restated accordingly.
  \item \textbf{X4 strict form would kill ambiguity (a).} If
    X4 demanded $c_k=1$ (exact reduction), then $\lambda=1$
    and the ``up to scale'' clause of Thm.~\ref{thm:main} is
    vacuous. The projective form is the consistent choice.
  \item \textbf{X6 conclusion-smuggling (referee attack
    sustained, axiom weakened).} The old X6 required reduction
    to ``the modified Wheeler--DeWitt constraint of (U4)'',
    whose statement contains $\alpha\widehat\Sigma_\delta$ ---
    i.e.\ it assumed on the clock--geometry cross-sector
    precisely what X1$+$X4 are supposed to derive. The
    weakened X6 above is a pure universality-class membership
    statement (Kaimakkamis--Sil), makes no reference to
    $\Sigma_\delta$, and demonstrably does independent work
    only in step (v) of Lemma~\ref{lem:overdetermination}.
  \item \textbf{X2 near-smuggling (assessed, kept, scoped).}
    X2 identifies the gravity generator's flow with the
    $\hat\omega$-modular flow; this is the main paper's
    thermal-time postulate, and relative to it X2 does the
    bulk of the gravity-sector work
    (Lemma~\ref{lem:gravity-rigidity} is then short). This is
    legitimate \emph{relative} uniqueness (as Poincar\'e
    covariance is for $P^\mu$), but the theorem must be
    advertised as ``unique given thermal time,'' not as
    deriving thermal time. The non-degeneracy clause
    ($h\notin\CC\id$) is required: without it X2 is vacuous on
    the II$_1$ sector and the conjunction X1--X7 loses
    gravity-sector selectivity entirely --- this is the new
    failure mode introduced by the II$_1$ reclassification,
    and it is exactly why X2 cannot be stated w.r.t.\ the
    trace.
  \item \textbf{Mutual consistency / non-vacuous
    conjunction.} The de~Sitter static-patch witness model of
    the main paper's (H6)/(U6) satisfies X1--X7: it is
    \emph{natively} II$_1$~\cite{CLPW2022}, so the
    reclassification improves rather than damages
    non-vacuity. $\widehat C=\widehat{\mathfrak C}_k$ itself
    satisfies all seven axioms on that model by construction.
  \item \textbf{X1's old form had a vacuous clause.} The
    commutator condition
    ``$[\widehat C,\widehat\Pi_{\rm c}+\id\otimes\id]=0$''
    contained an identity summand that commutes with
    everything; removed.
\end{itemize}
