# Overview

I initially embarked on this project out of a curiosity to understand our collective human experience.

I had a thought come to me one day while thinking about quantum entanglement and "spooky action at a distance"...

I thought, "What if its not two separate photons...what if its the same photon temporally linked through a quantum wormhole that emerges in a nonlinear crystal in a lab setting? So, what we think are two entangled photons are actually the same photon observed at the exact same time, with one entangled counterpart in the future and one entangled counterpart in the present, and that they mirror one another's spin because they must satisfy a past history condition? These photons come out of a nonlinear crystal with half the energy of the original, so why can't they simply be counterparts of the same original photon that was shot into the crystal, with some of its wave function partially tunneling through a quantum wormhole that emerges inside of that same crystal from the original photon particle that had the full original energy? What if the most modern instruments we have today are simply too slow to detect such a small delta time between the two counterparts?"

One thought lead to another...

"What if ER = EPR is true, what if black holes exist at the quantum level, and what if the universe has a lower limit on how much space matter can take up (try to compress matter further into that space and a black hole emerges), and an upper limit on how much space matter can fill (try to pack more matter into that space and a black hole emerges)? In essence, what if black holes and quantum wormholes are essentially just entropy preservers? In other words, a black hole emerges as if to prevent defying discrete limits in a region of space, and functions such that it must return the energy back to that same region of space at a later point in time..."

So, I set out to start vibe proofing a unification theory to try and derive General Relativity from these slightly different Quantum Mechanics principles...and its been a really interesting experience so far. Its gone far beyond what I thought could be achieved. To be completely honest, I *really* wanted to find a way to signal backwards in time from the future, only to find out that...it might actually not be possible to do so from within the framework itself.

The particularly distinctive part of this framework, by the way, is not simply that `unification.tex` derives `g = GM/R^2`. It is the attempt to place that derivation inside a larger common formalism combining:

* two-boundary or retrocausal quantum mechanics;
* modular or thermal time;
* operator-algebraic entanglement;
* an entropic derivation of semiclassical Einstein gravity;
* Wheeler–DeWitt-like constraints;
* a nonlocal advance-delay Schrödinger equation;
* final-boundary conditioning and branch selection.

I am not aware of a well-established framework that combines precisely that entire package into one master constraint, but individual ingredients and many of the reductions have substantial precedents.
